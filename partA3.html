##**Executive summary**

#It is known that Kidney transplant is often the treatment of choice to people with end-stage kidney disease. And it is important to understand the the characteristic of stable patients and patients who experienced rejection. This study is aimed to find out how well could us achieve in predicting  patient outcome in Kidney transplant that is whether stable or rejection as well as which genes are most important in predicting the patient outcome.

#To addressing the question interested, random forest model was selected to make predictions of patient outcomes. Random forest model is a well known and widely used machine learning approach which can handle both classification and regression problems. As the patient outcome in this study is a binary outcome which is stable or rejection, so it is a classification problem. For the classification task, suppose the total features is p, random Forests grows a large number of classification trees which is called 'forests' and each tree in the  'forests'  is generated based on a subset of features with a pre-defined number m where m < p. Then after the random forests are generated, when there are new samples input in the trained random forest model, each tree in the forest would accept the new samples as inputs and would generate predictions of outcomes for the new samples. Then given the classifications by each tree in the forests, the final prediction outcome by the random forest model is the outcome which is voted by the trees, for the classification problem, the final outcome is the mode of the predictions which is the class voted by the most of trees.

#And for this study,  the data used is GSE120396 which includes 88 samples(patients) with over 10000 gene expressions and the response outcome is whether these  patients stable or rejection in  Kidney transplant. Due to two many features(gene expressions), only gene expressions which have large variances among the samples were used in this study that over 1000 gene expressions were used as featues. Also, the obtain a robust outcome, 5-folds cross validation method was used with 25 repeatitions. The final result in the study obtained is that the average of the 25 repeated 5-folds cross validation accuracy in predicting patient come is about 0.725 with a standard error around 0.017. Thus, the accuracy obtained is not low which is over 70% and the standard error is very small so that it means the prediction results of random forest model should be very robust given new samples. So random forest performed well in predicting status of Kidney transplant for the patients, it could be used to predict the Kidney transplant status for patients who would accept Kidney transplant in future, if the prediction results of the patients were  turned out to be rejection, then the patients should consider the consequences seriously. And for finding the gene expressions which are most important in predicting the patient outcome, after building a random forest model on the full samples data, the importance of the gene expressions then computed by the variable importance measure average decrease in Gini coefficient if the gene expressions dropped.  The study found that the top 5 gene expressions are: RPH3A, SEMA6B, SIGLEC5, BATF2 and SERPING1. These 5 gene expressions have the most predict power in predicting the patient outcome whether would be stable or rejection. Thus, future studies could be focused on these 5 gene expressions to investigate the relations among them with the Kidney transplant which is very meaningful. 


#However, there are some potential shortcomings associated with the application of random forest model in predicting the patient outcome status with Kidney transplant.  The fist potential shortcoming is the selection of model parameters in the application, for example, how to find the optimal number of features used in each classification tree in the random forest. To deal with this issue, cross validations could be applied to tune the model parameters. The second potential shortcoming is that random forest model is hard to be applied on a large data set with high dimensions which is  common case for gene expressions data as  there are alway lots of gene expressions. Also, if the samples are large, it is even harder. A strategy to deal with this potential shortcoming is that using some dimension reducation methods, for example, PCA method could be very helpful.

set.seed(1)
cvK = 5  # number of CV folds
cv_50acc5_rf = c()
cv_acc_rf = c()
n_sim = 25 ## number of repeats
for (i in 1:n_sim) {

  cvSets = cvFolds(nrow(X), cvK)  # permute all the data, into 5 folds
  cv_acc_rf = c()
  for (j in 1:cvK) {
    test_id = cvSets$subsets[cvSets$which == j]
    X_test = X[test_id, ]
    X_train = X[-test_id, ]
    y_test = y[test_id]
    y_train = y[-test_id]
    ## RandomForest
    rf_res <-  randomForest(x = X_train, y = as.factor(y_train))
    fit <- predict(rf_res, X_test)
    cv_acc_rf[j] = table(fit, y_test) %>% diag %>% sum %>% `/`(length(y_test))
  }
  cv_50acc5_rf <- append(cv_50acc5_rf, mean(cv_acc_rf))
}
